The scientist who named the pattern

⭕ 🔺 ⬛

For centuries, math loved smooth shapes: circles, squares, triangles. But nature is not smooth — clouds are not circles, mountains are not triangles, and trees are definitely not squares.

👨‍🔬 ✨

In 1975, the mathematician Benoît Mandelbrot finally gave nature's rough, repeating shapes a name: fractals, from the Latin word fractus — "broken".

So, what is a fractal?

A fractal is a shape made of smaller copies of itself. Zoom into any piece and you find the same pattern again — smaller, but the same.
