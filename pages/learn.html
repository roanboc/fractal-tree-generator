How do fractals work?

A fractal is a picture made by following one simple rule, again and again and again. Each time you repeat the rule, the picture gets richer — that's called an iteration. Let's grow a tree, one iteration at a time!

The one and only rule

✏️

1. Draw a stick

Just one straight line. Easy!

✌️

2. Split the top

At the tip, draw two smaller sticks, one leaning left and one leaning right.

🔁

3. Repeat!

Do the same thing on every new stick. That's it. That's the whole trick.

The rule, written as a formula

Here is the entire recipe the computer follows — a rule that uses itself. That self-call is what mathematicians call recursion: 

tree(size) =
  draw a stick of length size
  tree(size × shrink)  ↖ tilted left
  tree(size × shrink)  ↗ tilted right

That's all! tree calls tree twice, each time with a smaller size, until the sticks get tiny. And since every stick makes two more, after n rounds you have drawn 2ⁿ − 1 sticks in total.

Why do real trees look messy?

Nature never repeats the rule perfectly. If we let every stick pick its own angle and size from a small range, the same rule grows a tree that looks alive — and no two are ever the same. That's exactly what the Wildness slider does in the generator.
