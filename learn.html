How do fractals work?

A fractal is a picture made by following one simple rule, again and again and again. Each time you repeat the rule, the picture gets richer — that's called an iteration. Let's grow a tree, one iteration at a time!

The one and only rule

✏️

1. Draw a stick

Just one straight line. Easy!

✌️

2. Split the top

At the tip, draw two smaller sticks, one leaning left and one leaning right.

🔁

3. Repeat!

Do the same thing on every new stick. That's it. That's the whole trick.

Why do real trees look messy?

Nature never repeats the rule perfectly. If we let every stick wiggle a tiny random amount, the same rule grows a tree that looks alive — and no two are ever the same.
